# Master Notes: Talk 1 – Harvard School Commencement Speech (1986)

## Talk Context

- **When:** June 13, 1986
- **Where:** Harvard School graduation ceremony
- **Why:** Munger invited as one of the oldest and longest-serving trustees
- **Audience:** Graduating students, parents, faculty
- **Theme:** Inversion as a life tool

## Core Argument

Instead of asking "How can I be happy?", ask "How can I guarantee misery?" and then avoid those things. This is the power of inversion — solving problems by turning them backward.

## Structure Outline

1. **Opening** — Why selected, speech length teaser
2. **Carson's 3 Prescriptions for Misery** — Chemicals, envy, resentment
3. **Munger's 4 Additional Prescriptions:**
   - Be unreliable
   - Learn only from personal experience
   - Stay down after setbacks
   - Ignore inversion/disconfirming evidence
4. **Darwin Example** — Extreme objectivity as the antidote
5. **Closing Toast** — "May each of you rise high by spending each day aiming low"

## Key Examples/Case Studies (from Munger)

1. **Johnny Carson's speech** — Original misery prescriptions (chemicals, envy, resentment)
2. **Munger's 4 friends** — 2 dead from alcohol, 1 living alcoholic (addiction subtle until too strong)
3. **Samuel Johnson** — Transcended envy, demonstrated it's possible
4. **Disraeli's compromise** — Gave up vengeance but kept names in drawer for private satisfaction
5. **Dyslexic college roommate** — Severely disadvantaged but reliable → became CEO of multibillion dollar company
6. **Croesus** — Richest king, later captive, quoted Solon: "No man's life happy until it's over"
7. **Isaac Newton** — Mastered predecessors' work, "stood on shoulders of giants"
8. **Epicetus** — Slave, maimed, poor, yet "favoured by Gods"
9. **Einstein** — Inverted the problem (revised Newton's laws to fit Maxwell's, not vice versa)
10. **Darwin** — Extreme objectivity, prioritized disconfirming evidence, outran the hares

## The 7 Prescriptions for Misery

### Carson's Original 3:
1. **Ingesting chemicals** — Addiction can happen to anyone; bonds too light until too strong
2. **Envy** — Wreaking havoc since before Moses
3. **Resentment** — "Life is hard enough to swallow without squeezing in the bitter rind"

### Munger's 4 Additions:
4. **Be unreliable** — Single habit that counters all virtues; you'll be outrun by mediocre turtles
5. **Learn only from personal experience** — Ignore others' mistakes and predecessors' wisdom
6. **Go down and stay down** — After first, second, third setback, stay defeated
7. **Ignore inversion** — Don't seek disconfirming evidence; protect original conclusions

## Inversion Themes (for Day 3)

**How to guarantee misery in life:**
- Use drugs/alcohol to alter mood
- Envy others' success
- Hold onto resentment
- Be unreliable
- Learn only from your own mistakes
- Give up after setbacks
- Avoid disconfirming evidence

**How to fail in career:**
- Be unreliable (most important single factor)
- Ignore what worked for successful predecessors
- Stay defeated after failures
- Protect your existing beliefs from challenge

**How to make bad decisions:**
- Never invert the problem
- Avoid vicarious learning
- Process information to confirm what you already believe

## Career Applications (for Day 4)

1. **Reliability is non-negotiable**
   - It can overcome other disadvantages (dyslexic roommate example)
   - Unreliability cancels out all other virtues
   - You'll be outrun by mediocre but reliable people

2. **Learn vicariously**
   - Study others' mistakes to avoid repeating them
   - Master the best work of predecessors
   - Don't limit yourself to personal experience

3. **Embrace extreme objectivity**
   - Actively seek evidence that disproves your theories
   - Darwin's method: prioritize disconfirming information
   - Einstein: test and destroy your own beloved ideas

4. **Persist through setbacks**
   - Epicetus: maimed, poor, slave — still "favoured by Gods"
   - Don't stay down after reverses
   - Adversity is inevitable even for the lucky and wise

5. **Use inversion in problem-solving**
   - Ask "How could this fail?" not just "How to succeed?"
   - Einstein inverted: revised Newton to fit Maxwell
   - Many hard problems solve best when addressed backward

## Key Takeaways

1. **Inversion** — Solve problems backward; ask "How to fail?" then avoid those things
2. **Reliability** — The single most important habit; without it, virtues don't matter
3. **Vicarious learning** — Learn from others' mistakes; don't limit yourself to personal experience
4. **Extreme objectivity** — Seek disconfirming evidence; test and destroy your own ideas
5. **Persistence** — Don't stay down after setbacks; adversity is normal
