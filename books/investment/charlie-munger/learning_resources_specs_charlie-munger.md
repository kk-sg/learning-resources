## 4. PROGRESSIVE DEPTH STRUCTURE

Each day builds on the previous, creating a progressive learning journey:

| Day   | Depth Level | Description                          | Connection to Previous                        |
| ----- | ----------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------- |
| Day 1 | Foundation  | What is this talk about?             | N/A (Start of week)                           |
| Day 2 | Models      | What mental models does it teach?    | Builds on Day 1 overview                      |
| Day 3 | Inversion   | How can we fail at this?             | Uses Day 2 models to find failure modes       |
| Day 4 | Application | How do we apply this professionally? | Applies Days 1–3 to career context            |
| Day 5 | Psychology  | What biases does this reveal?        | Connects models to psychology                 |
| Day 6 | Integration | How do we diagnose real cases?       | Uses all previous days to analyze cases       |
| Day 7 | Synthesis   | How do we internalize this?          | Integrates entire week into personal practice |

**Rule:** Each day should reference insights from previous days to maintain continuity.

### Multi-Week Talk Structure

For talks that span 2 or 3 weeks, each week follows the same Day 1–7 pattern, but with different content focus:

#### 2-Week Talks (Talks 2, 4, 9)

| Week   | Focus                  | Day 7 Review Purpose                       |
| ------ | ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Week 1 | First half of content  | Review first half, prepare for second half |
| Week 2 | Second half of content | Full synthesis of entire talk              |

**Week 1 Day 7:** Review and synthesis of Week 1 concepts
**Week 2 Day 7:** Full synthesis of the entire talk, integrating both weeks

#### 3-Week Talk (Talk 11 - Psychology of Human Misjudgment)

| Week   | Focus                           | Tendencies            | Day 7 Review Purpose             |
| ------ | ------------------------------- | --------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Week 1 | Foundation - Incentive & Social | #1–8                  | Review foundational tendencies   |
| Week 2 | Cognitive & Emotional Biases    | #9–17                 | Review cognitive biases          |
| Week 3 | Advanced & Integration          | #18–25 + Lollapalooza | Full synthesis + countermeasures |

**Week 1 Day 7:** Review tendencies 1–8 with examples
**Week 2 Day 7:** Review tendencies 9–17 with examples
**Week 3 Day 7:** Full synthesis of all 25 tendencies + personal countermeasure plan

### Multi-Week Continuity Rules

1. **Each week's Day 1** should recap the previous week's key insights (if applicable)
2. **Day 7 of final week** should synthesize the ENTIRE talk, not just that week
3. **File naming** tracks absolute week number, not week-within-talk
4. **Master notes** are created once at the start of the first week and referenced throughout

**Day-by-Day Breakdown:**
- Days 1-2: Deep dive into first 2 core models (Circle of Competence, Man with Hammer Syndrome)
- Days 3-4: Apply those 2 models to real-world scenarios
- Days 5-7: Integrate 2 core models into existing framework (latticework, circle of competence)

1. **Better retention** - Learners remember what they go deep into, not shallow overviews

3. **Reduced cognitive load** - Less overwhelming, more digestible
4. **Higher quality** - More nuanced understanding per model

**Content Length Target:** ~2,000-2,500 words per day (compared to ~1,000-1,200 for breadth)
